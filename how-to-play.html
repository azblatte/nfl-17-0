17–0

How to Play

The goal

The NFL regular season is 17 games. Only one team in history finished a season truly perfect (the 1972 Dolphins). Your job: assemble an all-time roster strong enough — and balanced enough — to run the table at 17-0 and then win it all.

Your roster

You fill six spots, in any order:

  • QB · RB · WR · WR · TE · Head Coach

How drafting works

A slot machine rolls a random team and a random decade (1960s through 2020s). That franchise's whole offense from that era — plus its head coach — appears with their stats. Click the player you want to drop them into a matching open slot. Positions you've already filled are locked and shown dimmed.

Every roll is independent — you can land the same team or decade more than once. After each pick, the machine spins again until your six spots are full.

Skips

  • Team Skip — reroll to a different franchise (you get one per game).
  • Decade Skip — reroll to a different era (you get one per game).

Land a weak team-era and these are how you chase an all-timer instead. Spend them wisely.

How wins are decided

Every player carries an era-adjusted greatness score built from their real production, so a 1960s legend and a modern superstar are judged fairly against their own times. Your roster's projection rewards balance: the win curve is nonlinear, and your single weakest spot drags everything down. A deficiency at even one position can cost you a perfect season.

Playoffs & the 20-0 grail

Win at least 10 games and you make the playoffs. Finish 15+ and you earn the #1 seed and a first-round bye. From there you run the gauntlet — Wild Card, Divisional, Conference Championship, Super Bowl — with each round tougher than the last and your odds set by your roster's strength.

The ultimate prize: a perfect 17-0 season that earns the #1 seed, then three straight playoff wins for a 20-0 championship — the immortal run no NFL team has ever truly had.

Game modes

  • Classic — every player's stats and greatness score are shown.
  • GridIQ — stats are hidden. You draft on football knowledge alone, and the full roster is graded at the end.
